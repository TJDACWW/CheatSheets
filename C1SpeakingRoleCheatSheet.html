C1 Speaking Roles Cheat Sheet
Conversation personalities for pair speaking practice
Interrupts Often
Behaviour
  • Interrupt fairly often to add ideas.
  • React quickly to what your partner says.
  • Redirect the discussion slightly when useful.
Say
"Sorry to interrupt, but ..."
"Can I just add something there?"
"Before you move on, ..."
"Actually, I’d say ..."
Indecisive
Behaviour
  • Sound hesitant and uncertain.
  • Weigh up different options.
  • Delay making a clear decision.
Say
"I’m in two minds about that."
"It’s hard to say, really."
"Part of me thinks ..., but at the same time ..."
"I can see both sides, to be honest."
Always Asks Follow-Up Questions
Behaviour
  • Focus on extending your partner’s ideas.
  • Ask questions that develop the discussion.
  • Keep your own answers brief.
Say
"What makes you say that?"
"How would that work in practice?"
"Do you think that’s always the case?"
"Could you give an example of that?"
Always Politely Disagrees
Behaviour
  • Challenge ideas in a calm, respectful way.
  • Acknowledge the point before disagreeing.
  • Give a reason for your view.
Say
"I can see your point, but I’m not sure I agree."
"That’s a fair point, although ..."
"I take what you mean, but I’d argue that ..."
"I’m not entirely convinced, because ..."
Summarise Before Adding
Behaviour
  • Briefly restate your partner’s idea.
  • Show that you have listened carefully.
  • Then build on the point.
Say
"So, if I understand you correctly, ..."
"What you’re saying is that ..."
"You seem to be arguing that ..."
"Building on that, I’d say ..."
Ask for Clarification
Behaviour
  • Ask for more detail when something is unclear.
  • Push your partner to explain their meaning.
  • Focus on precision.
Say
"What exactly do you mean by that?"
"Could you clarify what you mean?"
"I’m not sure I quite follow you."
"Would you mind explaining that a bit further?"
Minimalist
Behaviour
  • Keep answers short, but still contribute something meaningful.
  • Add a brief reason or example.
  • Leave space for your partner to continue.
Say
"I’d say yes, mainly because it’s more practical."
"Possibly, though it depends on the context."
"Not necessarily, but I can see why people think that."
"I’m not fully convinced, to be honest."
Speculator
Behaviour
  • Avoid sounding too certain.
  • Explore possibilities and alternatives.
  • Use tentative language naturally.
Say
"It might be a question of ..."
"I suppose it could be because ..."
"Perhaps people tend to ..."
"One possible explanation is that ..."
Unbiased
Behaviour
  • Present both sides of the argument.
  • Avoid taking a strong position too early.
  • End with a balanced conclusion.
Say
"There are valid arguments on both sides."
"On the one hand ..., whereas on the other ..."
"It largely depends on the individual and the situation."
"Overall, I’d say there isn’t a simple answer."
Skeptic
Behaviour
  • Question assumptions and claims.
  • Challenge whether ideas are realistic or well supported.
  • Ask your partner to justify their view.
Say
"I’m not sure that really holds true in practice."
"That sounds plausible, but is it actually realistic?"
"I’d question whether that would work for most people."
"What makes you think that would be effective?"
