B2 Speaking Roles
Conversation personalities for pair speaking practice
Interrupts Often
Behaviour
  • Interrupt to add ideas.
  • React quickly.
  • Change direction slightly.
Useful phrases
"Sorry, can I say something?"
"Just a second …"
"Actually …"
"I think …"
Indecisive
Behaviour
  • Show uncertainty.
  • Change your opinion.
  • Consider both options.
Useful phrases
"I'm not sure …"
"Maybe …, but also …"
"It could be …, but …"
"I can't decide."
Follow-Up Questioner
Behaviour
  • Ask many questions.
  • Keep your partner talking.
  • Give short answers.
Useful phrases
"Why?"
"What do you mean?"
"Can you give an example?"
"What about you?"
Polite Disagreer
Behaviour
  • Disagree respectfully.
  • Show understanding first.
Useful phrases
"I understand, but …"
"I see your point, however …"
"Maybe, but I don't agree."
"I'm not sure about that."
Summariser
Behaviour
  • Repeat your partner's idea.
  • Add your own opinion afterwards.
Useful phrases
"So you think …"
"You said that …"
"I agree with that, and …"
"That's interesting, I think …"
Clarifier
Behaviour
  • Ask when you don't understand.
  • Encourage more explanation.
Useful phrases
"Can you explain?"
"What do you mean?"
"I don't understand."
"Can you say that again?"
Minimalist
Behaviour
  • Give short answers.
  • Add one small reason.
  • Let your partner continue.
Useful phrases
"Yes, I think so."
"Maybe, it depends."
"I'm not sure, but …"
"I don't really like it."
Speculator
Behaviour
  • Talk about possibilities.
  • Avoid sounding too certain.
Useful phrases
"Perhaps …"
"It might be …"
"Maybe …"
"I think it could be …"
Balanced Thinker
Behaviour
  • Discuss both sides.
  • Keep the conversation open.
Useful phrases
"On one hand …, but on the other hand …"
"It can be good, but also bad."
"Both are possible."
"In general, I'd say …"
Skeptic
Behaviour
  • Question ideas politely.
  • Ask for more explanation.
  • Express doubt with reasons.
Useful phrases
"I'm not sure that's true …"
"That might be difficult."
"Do people really do that?"
"Why do you think that works?"
